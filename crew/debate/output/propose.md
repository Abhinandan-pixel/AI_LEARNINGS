Social media platforms should legally ban users under the age of 16 to protect mental health because extensive research shows that early and excessive exposure to these platforms significantly increases risks of anxiety, depression, and self-esteem issues among adolescents. The developing brains of children under 16 are particularly vulnerable to the negative effects of cyberbullying, social comparison, and addictive behaviors promoted by social media algorithms. By legally restricting access until a more mature age, we reduce their exposure during critical developmental years, giving them time to build healthy coping mechanisms and self-identity offline. This targeted protection would act like age restrictions in other harmful domains, such as alcohol or tobacco, recognizing that mental health requires similar safeguards. Ultimately, a legal ban for under-16s is a responsible, evidence-based public health measure to curb the rising tide of youth mental health crises linked to social media use.
